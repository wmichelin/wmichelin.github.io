Greetings, my name is Walter Michelin and I'm a Senior Computer Science major at The College of New Jersey. I have been interning with Infragistics since May 2014 in their services department, working on various web applications.

Some of the technologies I have worked with are:
  • HTML/CSS/Javascript
  • JQuery
  • Knockout JS
  • ASP.NET MVC

In my free time, I enjoy working with node.js and the various libraries available on the node platform.

In my time at The College of New Jersey I have participated in many hackathons, although I would say I used my time at these events learning, instead of competing. I am always looking to sharpen my skills by learning new technologies and design patterns.

My favorite project in school so far has been in my information retrieval course. I created a full stack search engine in node.js, and this code is available on my GitHub account.

This spring I will be conducting mentored research under Dr. Knox and Dr. DePasquale, creating a full stack web application for The College of New Jersey. More information about this project will be made available upon request by contacting me at wmichelin@gmail.com.

I am 21 years old, and I am from Seattle, Washington. I currently reside in Ewing, New Jersey. I am going to graduate from The College of New Jersey with a degree in Computer Science in December 2015.

I have been programming since I was 15 years old. I discovered programming at Toms River High School South, during my sophomore year of high school by taking an introductory level computer science course. We started out using Borland C++ and learning the most basic elements of programming. I moved on the AP Computer Science course the following year, which expanded on our knowledge, as well as introduced us to some OOP concepts such as classes, polymorphism, etc.

After high school, I wasn't entirely sure what I wanted to do with my life, but I thought it had something to do with business. I attended Ocean County College from 2011 to 2013, where I recieved an associates degree in Business Administration. I transferred to The College of New Jersey, where I found out I wanted to become a software developer. So far in my time here I have grown immensely as a programmer, although I still have a lot to learn.
